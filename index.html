by Brennan Tanner

Introduction

In this project, I attempted to redesign the UI of the classic video game Mother 3. This game holds a special place in the hearts of many, known for its compelling narrative and memorable characters. I wanted to create a UI that respected its roots while catering to today's gaming expectations, breathing new life into this timeless gem.

The origial UI

My journey started with a deep dive into the original game's UI. Understanding its mechanics, player interactions, and visual style was crucial. I gathered insights from fellow gamers and community discussions, understanding what elements resonated most and where improvements could be made. Analyzing current gaming trends and UI standards guided me in crafting a UI that felt both familiar and fresh.

Personas

Creating a persona can be one of the trickiest parts of a good design process. Bad designers will design for them, when good designers design for their persona. So the tricky question is: Who are these users and what are their like and dislikes?

Kelly Sanders

Kelly is a curious and cautious parent who wants to check out video games herself to ensure they are a fun safe environment for her kid to play. She has only played a few games before now, but she is quick to pick up controls.

Button Mapping

What affordances does the controller allow? Before designing, I decided to break down the nintendo switch controller into sections to figure out which buttons are even allowed. Because sure, if there are 8 actions a user can take, I could just systematically map each action to a different button. But due to common conventions, that would only frustrate users as they are forced re-learn how to play video games.

Key takeaways

The two buttons that are most important are A and B, almost every action should be reached using these two. But at the same time, I do not want to drown users in sub-menus. so X an Y will help ease that burden from time to time. every other button will be basically be used for navigation.

Final Thoughts

Take a moment to look through the slides. They are in order so you can imagine the flow of using an item or choosing an attack.

I decided to use a transparent, glassy menu, much like we saw in the Mario RPG study. I think it gives an element of "sci-fi" in this goofy world, as that is a big theme in the game

Notice the square design throughout, except for the action choosing menu in battle. I wanted to keep this circular astethic agaist my better judgement because I thought is looked so cool, plus games like Mario Superstar Saga and persona 5 really inspired me to have some sort of interactive menu system for battle, This could use some tweaking still I think, but overall it looks good enough for now.
